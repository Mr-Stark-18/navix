The problem

Sourcing from India's long tail is a trust problem.

The demand is here. The makers are here. What's missing is a reliable way to know who can deliver, and the plumbing to run the order once you do.

A fragmented map

Thousands of mostly family-owned units across ceramics, textiles, decor and furniture. Around 60% of Morbi alone is unorganized. Finding the right maker is a needle in a haystack.

No neutral way to know who to trust

Without primary diligence, a reliable factory looks the same as a risky one. So buyers lean on a handful of known names and miss the makers who'd serve them better.

Defects and delays are routine

Breakage in transit, shade and calibre variation, missed timelines. Disputes eat into margin and wear down good relationships on both sides.

Working capital stays tight

Makers are under-financed and the people sourcing from them carry the exposure. Capital is mispriced because real performance is invisible.

There's no neutral system-of-record for who, in the long tail, can be trusted. That's the gap SPEQULA is built to close, starting with what can be measured on the ground.
